# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Institutional personnel at Filamer Christian University (QA Controllers, Office Staff, VPs, and the President). Users are often busy and need to process high volumes of document tasks with precision, often under time pressure.

## Product Purpose
A centralized document operations and control system (FilDOCS) designed to streamline institutional workflows, ensure compliance, and provide real-time status tracking for document revisions and approvals.

## Brand Personality
Modern, Professional, User Friendly. The system should feel like a high-end productivity tool (similar to Linear or Stripe) rather than a corporate legacy portal or a playful consumer app.

## Anti-references
- **Vibe-coded UI**: Avoid excessive shadows, blurry edges, and oversized rounded corners (rounded-3xl).
- **Inconsistent Color**: No "purpley" gradients or colors that deviate from the established sky-blue brand theme.
- **Legacy Clutter**: Avoid the "old school website" look with cramped layouts, low contrast, or complex navigation.
- **Shadow-based Separation**: Use borders and spacing for hierarchy instead of soft drop shadows.

## Design Principles
- **Work Queue First**: The interface must prioritize the discovery and completion of pending tasks. Content should never bury the action.
- **Utilitarian Precision**: Every pixel must serve a purpose. Design for clarity and high information density.
- **Zero Decorative Noise**: Colors, animations, and shapes must indicate status or hierarchy, never just "decoration."
- **Institutional Confidence**: The UI must feel stable and reliable, using a strict grid and consistent geometry to build user trust.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- **High Contrast Utility**: Ensure all text and status indicators exceed WCAG AA contrast ratios (4.5:1 for normal text).
- **Clear Focus State**: Every interactive element must have a highly visible, high-contrast focus ring for keyboard navigation.
- **Semantic Clarity**: Use proper HTML5 elements (nav, aside, main) to ensure compatibility with screen readers and assistive tech.
- **Reduced Motion Support**: All animations must respect the `prefers-reduced-motion` system preference.
